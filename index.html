Change in Temperature

The Global Mean Surface Temperature (GMST) fluctuates quite variably on a yearly frame of reference, and this characteristic of GMST is often sensationalised in the media. Due to the volatility of GMST, data over short periods does not convey valuable information. This graph shows us the general trend of GMST from 1961 to 2021. The general trend is an increase in GMST as temperature increases are more frequent and have a more significant magnitude year over year. If this trend continues, we will be looking at a GMST increase of ≅1.83℃ by the year 2050.

Change in CO2 Levels

Concentrations of carbon dioxide change significantly on the scale of centuries or even millennia. Since 1961, concentrations have followed an exponential uptrend, going from around 317ppm to 426ppm. If this exponential trend in CO2 concentrations stays unchanged, projections for the year 2050 hint towards upwards of ≅472 parts per million. Carbon dioxide is a reiterating variable, which means that it amplifies the positive feedback loop of the enhanced greenhouse effect.

Sea Levels and Aciditiy

This graph shows us a significant increase in sea levels in just 20 years, almost 10cm. This is an average rise of 3.6mm per year, which will likely increase given the nature of the exponential growth of CO2 concentrations in the atmosphere. This graph is much less volatile than the others, evidenced by the fact that the general uptrend is easy to decipher even without a trendline. Decreases in sea levels are very rare and do not have much magnitude, whereas the increase in sea levels is prominent. Rising sea levels affect humans living in coastal areas due to the looming threat of coastal inundation and floods. Coastal inundation also destroys coastal animal habitats and upsets their fragile ecosystems.
