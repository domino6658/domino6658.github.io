Temperature Increase

The Global Mean Surface Temperature (GMST) fluctuates quite variably on a yearly frame of reference, and this characteristic of GMST is often sensationalised in the media. Due to the volatility of GMST, data over short periods does not convey valuable information. This graph shows us the general trend of GMST from 1961 to 2021. The general trend is an increase in GMST as temperature increases are more frequent and have a more significant magnitude year over year. If this trend continues, we will be looking at a GMST increase of ≅1.83℃ by the year 2050.

Heatmap

This is a heatmap of each country’s average temperature change based on pre-industrialisation levels. Across all countries with data, there was an average increase of 1.382°c from 1961 to 2022, with the lowest value being -2.062°c in Greenland in 1983 and the highest being 3.691°c in Russia in 2020. The data shows that every country had an increasing annual average temperature. The country with the least increase was the Pitcairn Islands, with a decrease of -0.106°c. The country with the most significant growth was Luxembourg, with 1.584°c. This data is used to understand the global trends in temperature change and find the most important contributors to this increase in temperature.
